Clear value proposition – Visitors should immediately understand what the company offers and how it differs from competitors. This includes listing the types of properties managed, key services, and geographic coverage.

Mobile responsiveness – Around 60 % of web traffic comes from mobile devices, so the site must scale gracefully on phones and tablets. Buttons and forms should be easy to tap, and images should resize without distortion.

Portal functionality – Clear login flows for both residents (tenants) and investors/owners reduce friction in paying rent, submitting maintenance requests and viewing financial reports. Dual portals are essential because property‑management websites must serve both owners and residents.

Content strategy – Strong blogs, podcasts, guides, checklists and FAQs build trust and help with SEO. Content should address the questions of both residents and investors.

Dual‑audience approach – Because the site serves owners and residents, sections and navigation should be clearly segmented for each audience.

These principles will inform the analysis of competitor sites and inspire recommendations for KAZA Booking.
